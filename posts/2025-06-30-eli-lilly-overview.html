Eli Lilly and Company (LLY), established in 1876, is a centennial global pharmaceutical powerhouse dedicated to developing and marketing medicines and vaccines across various therapeutic areas, including Alzheimer's disease, diabetes, oncology, metabolic disorders, and autoimmune diseases.

The company boasts a diversified product portfolio featuring market-leading drugs such as Trulicity (dulaglutide) for diabetes, Verzenio (abemaciclib) for breast cancer, and Taltz for psoriasis. However, its current growth is primarily driven by its blockbuster GLP-1 receptor agonists: Mounjaro (tirzepatide) and Zepbound (tirzepatide).

Lilly's current market valuation reflects strong investor confidence, largely due to its competitive advantage in the weight loss and type 2 diabetes markets. While the entry of competitors beyond Novo Nordisk poses a challenge to maintaining market share over the next three years, Lilly is aggressively expanding its pipeline beyond weight management.

History and Key Milestones

Eli Lilly was founded in 1876 by Colonel Eli Lilly, a pharmaceutical chemist and Civil War veteran. After serving in the Union Army (1861–1865), he worked in several pharmacies before establishing his own manufacturing business.

Early Development and Innovation (1876 – 1940s)

  • 1876: Colonel Eli Lilly founded the company after a brief partnership with John F. Johnston. The initial focus was on high-quality drugs, including quinine for malaria.
  • 1880s: Introduced innovations such as gelatin-coated pills/capsules and fruit-flavored or sugar-coated tablets for easier ingestion.
  • 1923: Commercialization of Insulin. In collaboration with the University of Toronto, Lilly became the first company to mass-produce insulin (Iletin), establishing its leadership in diabetes care.
  • 1940s: Pioneered the mass production of penicillin for Allied forces during World War II.

Post-War Expansion (1950s – 1980s)

  • 1952: Became a publicly traded company.
  • 1954: Entered animal health by establishing Elanco Products Company.
  • 1955: Became one of the first manufacturers of the Salk polio vaccine.
  • 1961: Developed its first oncology drug.
  • 1971: Acquired Elizabeth Arden (cosmetics), later sold in 1987.
  • 1977: Expanded into medical devices by acquiring IVAC and Cardiac Pacemakers.

Biotech Rise and Neuroscience Breakthroughs (1990s – Present)

  • 1982: Launched Humulin® (human insulin), becoming one of the first companies to use recombinant DNA technology, cementing its diabetes leadership.
  • 1986/1987: Launched Prozac® (fluoxetine). This selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI) revolutionized depression treatment and established Lilly as a major player in neuroscience.

Modern Challenges and Transformation (2000s – Present)

  • 2011: Formed an alliance with Boehringer Ingelheim to develop diabetes drugs, including Jardiance®.
  • 2014: Acquired Novartis’ animal health business to strengthen Elanco (fully spun off in 2019).
  • 2020: Rapidly developed monoclonal antibody therapies in response to the COVID-19 pandemic.
  • 2022 – 2025: Executed a series of strategic acquisitions, including Akouos, DICE Therapeutics, Sigilon Therapeutics, Versanis Bio, Mablink Biosciences, POINT Biopharma, Morphic Therapeutic, and Scorpion Therapeutics.
  • 2024: Kisunla™ (donanemab) approved for early Alzheimer's disease; Ebglyss® (lebrikizumab) approved for atopic dermatitis.
  • 2025: Continued focus on acquisitions and manufacturing capacity expansion.

Pharmaceutical Product Portfolio

Eli Lilly maintains a robust pipeline addressing significant unmet medical needs.

Top-Selling Products

  • Mounjaro (tirzepatide): Dual GLP-1/GIP receptor agonist for type 2 diabetes. Generated $11.54 billion in 2024. Projected to become the world's best-selling drug by 2030 ($36 billion).
  • Zepbound (tirzepatide): Indicated for weight management and Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA). Launched in late 2023, it rapidly accumulated $4.93 billion in 2024. Evaluate Pharma predicts sales of $25.5 billion by 2030.
  • Trulicity (dulaglutide): GLP-1 receptor agonist generating $7.1 billion in 2024. Patent expires in 2027.
  • Verzenio (abemaciclib): CDK4/6 inhibitor for breast cancer ($5.31 billion in 2024).
  • Jardiance (empagliflozin): SGLT2 inhibitor generating $3.34 billion in 2024 (+7.4% YoY).

Recent Approvals

  • Kisunla™ (donanemab-azbt): Anti-amyloid beta mAb approved in July 2024 for Alzheimer's.
  • Tirzepatide (New Indication): Reduced heart failure outcomes by 38% in HFpEF patients with obesity (SUMMIT trial). Applications submitted to FDA/EMA.
  • Omvoh (mirikizumab): IL-23 antagonist for ulcerative colitis and Crohn’s disease.
  • EBGLYSS (lebrikizumab): IL-13 antagonist for atopic dermatitis.

Phase 3 Candidates with High Potential

  • Orforglipron: Oral GLP-1 receptor agonist. Phase 3 ACHIEVE-1 showed significant efficacy. Filing expected in 2026.
  • Remternetug: Next-generation anti-Aβ mAb for Alzheimer's.

Early-Stage Candidates

  • Lepodisiran: siRNA targeting LPA. Phase 2 data showed 94% reduction in Lp(a) with potential for infrequent dosing (up to 1.5 years).
  • Muvalaplin: Oral small molecule inhibiting Lp(a) formation. Phase 2 showed up to 85.8% reduction.

Patent Expirations (Loss of Exclusivity)

  • Trulicity (2027): Impact mitigated by Tirzepatide uptake.
  • Cyramza (2026): 2024 sales were $973.3 million.
  • Taltz: Biologics data protection expires in 2028; compound patent in 2030.

Note: Biologics Data Protection (12 years in the US) prevents competitors from using the originator's data for biosimilar applications. Compound Patents (20 years) prevent the manufacture/sale of the specific molecule.

Strategic Acquisitions and M&A

Lilly utilizes a "string-of-pearls" strategy to access cutting-edge technologies.

Key Acquisitions in First Half of 2025:

  • Scorpion Therapeutics (STX-478): Acquired for up to $2.5 billion. Mutant-selective PI3Kα inhibitor for breast cancer.
  • SiteOne Therapeutics (STC-004): Acquired for up to $1 billion. Non-opioid pain reliever targeting Nav1.8.
  • Verve Therapeutics (VERVE-102): Acquired for up to $1.3 billion. Base-editing gene therapy for ASCVD targeting PCSK9.

Competitive Landscape

Obesity & Diabetes

Primary Competitor: Novo Nordisk (Semaglutide).
Zepbound shows superior weight loss efficacy, but Novo has an oral semaglutide (Rybelsus) in Phase 3 for obesity.

Oncology (PI3Kα Inhibitors)

Primary Competitor: Novartis (Alpelisib).
Lilly's STX-478 is mutant-selective and a monotherapy candidate, potentially avoiding metabolic toxicities associated with Alpelisib.

Cholesterol / Lp(a)

Lilly's Lepodisiran differentiates from Amgen and Silence Therapeutics via its long duration of action (up to 1.5 years). While the PCSK9 market is crowded, Lilly’s therapies address distinct risk factors.

Atopic Dermatitis

EBGLYSS faces stiff competition from Sanofi's Dupixent, as well as oral JAK inhibitors (Cibinqo, Olumiant) and topical options (Opzelura).

Summary

Eli Lilly and Company remains a dominant force in the pharmaceutical industry, anchored by its historical leadership in diabetes and its current dominance with Mounjaro and Zepbound. With 2024 revenue growing 32% and net income doubling, the company is aggressively reinvesting in manufacturing and R&D.

Despite looming patent cliffs for legacy drugs like Trulicity, Lilly is successfully diversifying through strategic bolt-on acquisitions in oncology, gene editing, and immunology. The advancement of high-potential assets like the oral GLP-1 Orforglipron and the long-acting Lepodisiran positions Lilly to sustain its growth momentum and leadership in medical innovation for the coming decade.
