Eli Lilly and Company (LLY), founded in 1876, is a centennial global pharmaceutical leader dedicated to discovering and marketing therapies for a wide range of conditions, including Alzheimer's disease, diabetes, cancer, metabolic disorders, and autoimmune diseases. The company boasts a diverse portfolio featuring established leaders like Trulicity (dulaglutide), the breast cancer therapy Verzenio (abemaciclib), and the psoriasis treatment Taltz.

Currently, its growth is driven by the highly successful GLP-1 receptor agonists Mounjaro (tirzepatide) and Zepbound (tirzepatide). Lilly's current market valuation reflects strong investor confidence, primarily anchored in its competitive advantage in the weight loss market. Despite increasing competition—most notably from Novo Nordisk—Lilly is aggressively expanding its pipeline beyond weight management.

History and Key Milestones

Founded in 1876 by Colonel Eli Lilly, a pharmacist and Civil War veteran, the company began as a small pharmaceutical manufacturing operation.

Early Innovation (1876–1940s)

  • 1876: Founded with a focus on high-quality medicines, such as quinine for malaria.
  • 1880s: Introduced gelatin-coated pills and fruit-flavoured liquid medicines to improve palatability.
  • 1923: Commercialization of Insulin. Collaborated with the University of Toronto to become the first company to mass-produce insulin (Iletin), cementing its leadership in diabetes care.
  • 1940s: Pioneered mass production methods for penicillin during WWII.

Expansion and Diversification (1950s–1980s)

  • 1952: Became a publicly traded company.
  • 1954: Entered animal health with the formation of Elanco Products Company.
  • 1955: One of the first companies to manufacture and distribute the Salk polio vaccine.
  • 1982: Launched Humulin® (human insulin), the first healthcare product created using recombinant DNA technology.
  • 1986/1987: Launched Prozac® (fluoxetine), an SSRI that revolutionized depression treatment.

Modern Era (2000s–Present)

  • 2011: Formed a strategic alliance with Boehringer Ingelheim for diabetes (e.g., Jardiance®).
  • 2020: Rapidly developed monoclonal antibody therapies for COVID-19.
  • 2022–2025: Executed a "string-of-pearls" acquisition strategy, purchasing companies such as Akouos, DICE Therapeutics, Versanis Bio, Mablink Biosciences, POINT Biopharma, and Scorpion Therapeutics.
  • 2024: FDA approval of Kisunla™ (donanemab) for early symptomatic Alzheimer's and Ebglyss® (lebrikizumab) for atopic dermatitis.

Drug Product Portfolio

Lilly’s R&D focuses on addressing unmet needs in critical therapeutic areas.

Lilly's Revenue Split by Key Products

Blockbuster Products

  • Mounjaro (tirzepatide): A dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist for type 2 diabetes. Generated $11.54 billion in 2024 sales. Analysts project it could become the world's best-selling drug by 2030 ($36 billion).
  • Zepbound (tirzepatide): Indicated for weight management. Approved in late 2023, it rapidly accumulated $4.93 billion in 2024 sales. Expanded FDA approval in 2024 for obstructive sleep apnea (OSA).
  • Trulicity (dulaglutide): A foundational GLP-1 agonist. Despite patent expiry pressures (2027), it generated $7.1 billion in 2024.
  • Verzenio (abemaciclib): A CDK4/6 inhibitor for HR+, HER2- breast cancer ($5.31 billion in 2024).
  • Jardiance (empagliflozin): An SGLT2 inhibitor for diabetes and heart failure ($3.34 billion in 2024).

Recent Approvals and Near-Term Pipeline

  • Kisunla™ (donanemab-azbt): Anti-amyloid beta (Aβ) mAb approved in July 2024 for early Alzheimer’s disease.
  • Tirzepatide (HFpEF Label Expansion): Phase 3 SUMMIT trial showed a 38% reduction in heart failure events. Submissions to FDA/EMA are under review.
  • Omvoh (mirikizumab): IL-23 antagonist for ulcerative colitis and Crohn’s disease.
  • Ebglyss (lebrikizumab): IL-13 antagonist for atopic dermatitis.

Key Phase 3 and Early-Stage Candidates

Key Clinical Pipeline Assets

  • Orforglipron: An oral GLP-1 receptor agonist. Phase 3 ACHIEVE-1 data shows efficacy comparable to injectable GLP-1s. Filing expected in 2026.
  • Lepodisiran: siRNA therapy targeting the LPA gene. Phase 2 data indicates reduction of Lipoprotein(a) by 94% with a duration up to 1.5 years.
  • Muvalaplin: Oral small molecule inhibiting Lp(a) formation.

Strategic Acquisitions (2025 Focus)

Lilly utilizes a "bolt-on" acquisition strategy to acquire specific assets or technologies.

  • Scorpion Therapeutics: Acquired for up to $2.5 billion. Gained STX-478, a mutant-selective PI3Kα inhibitor for breast cancer.
  • SiteOne Therapeutics: Acquired for up to $1 billion. Gained STC-004, a non-opioid pain therapeutic (Nav1.8 inhibitor).
  • Verve Therapeutics: Acquired for up to $1.3 billion. Includes VERVE-102, a base-editing gene therapy for ASCVD targeting PCSK9.

Financial Performance

Lilly delivered exceptional financial results in recent quarters, driven by insatiable demand for obesity and diabetes treatments.

  • FY 2024: Revenue grew 32% to $45.04 billion. Net income surged 102% to $10.59 billion.
  • Q1 2025: Global revenue reached $12.73 billion (+45% YoY).
    • Mounjaro: $3.84 billion (+112.7% YoY)

Competitive Landscape

Obesity & Diabetes

Novo Nordisk (Semaglutide): The primary competitor with Wegovy/Ozempic. Lilly’s Zepbound has shown superior weight loss efficacy in head-to-head comparisons. Novo is advancing oral semaglutide (Rybelsus).

Oncology (PI3Kα Inhibitors)

Novartis (Piqray): Targets both mutant and wild-type PI3Kα, causing hyperglycemia. Lilly’s STX-478 is mutant-selective, potentially offering a better safety profile.

Cardiovascular (Lp(a) & PCSK9)

Lilly faces competition from Amgen (Olpasiran) and Silence Therapeutics in the siRNA space. However, Lilly’s Lepodisiran differentiates with a potentially longer duration of action (1.5 years). In the PCSK9 space, Lilly’s acquisition of Verve targets a "one-and-done" gene editing cure, distinct from chronic dosing injectables like Repatha.

Summary

Eli Lilly and Company remains a dominant force in the pharmaceutical industry. With Mounjaro and Zepbound poised to lead the global market, 2024 saw revenue jump 32%. Despite upcoming patent cliffs for older drugs like Trulicity, Lilly’s aggressive acquisition strategy in oncology, immunology, and genetic medicine—combined with high-potential pipeline assets like oral GLP-1s (Orforglipron)—positions the company for sustained long-term growth.
